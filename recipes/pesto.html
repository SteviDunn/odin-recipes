P is for Pesto

Question: Is pesto a sauce????

Answer: YES

This is Pesto. It tastes like garlic, nuts and basil (becauuse that's what it is).

Description:

Pesto was invented in Rome, Italy and originally used walnuts as the nut of choice. Today, I like to use roasted pine nuts in my recipe.

Making pesto is an art. One must find the best basil leaves from their local grocer. Make sure the leaves are not wilted! Basil is easy to grow at home too.

Pine nuts, on the other hand, are very difficult to source yourself becasue it is hard to extract them from their cone. Squirrels can eat pine nuts easily by destroying the entire cone and leaving the droppings below on the forest floor.

Olive oil can be purchased at the store. I suggest using McEvoy Ranch olive oil grown and bottled in Napa, CA. Their olive oil is rich, pungent and tastes authentically Italian (I assume; I've never been to Italy...).

Ingredients:

  • Olive oil
  • Basil
  • Garlic
  • Salt & Pepper
  • Pine Nuts
  • Cumin
  • Lemon

Steps

  1. Gather the ingredients listed above
  2. Make sure you have a blender for emulsification
  3. Add in the basil, garlic (amount up to your discretion), pine nuts and olive oil
  4. Pulse the blade on high and low whilst adding in olive oil
  5. Add lemon, salt, pepper and cumin to taste
  6. Repeat steps 3-5 until you like the taste of your pesto
  7. Serve on pasta, crackers or other dipping foods!
